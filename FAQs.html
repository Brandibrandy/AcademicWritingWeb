Citation & Formatting

How do I cite sources correctly?

Use the appropriate citation style such as APA, MLA, or Harvard, and include author, year, title, and source info.

What's the difference between APA and MLA?

APA emphasizes year of publication, MLA emphasizes page number. Both have different formatting guidelines.

How do I format in-text citations?

APA uses (Author, Year), MLA uses (Author Page#).

Where do I place the reference list?

At the end of your paper on a new page, labeled “References” or “Works Cited.”

Can I cite a website?

Yes, include author (or site name), date, title, and URL.

Do I italicize book titles?

Yes, italicize book and journal titles in APA, MLA, and Harvard.

How do I cite a YouTube video?

Include uploader, date, title, format, and URL.

What is a hanging indent?

A formatting style where the first line is flush left and subsequent lines are indented.

Should I include access dates?

APA does not require it; MLA and Harvard do for web sources.

What's the correct font and spacing?

Times New Roman, 12pt, double-spaced is the standard.

Can I mix citation styles?

No, stick to one style throughout your document.

Is there a citation generator I can use?

Yes, many online tools like Zotero or EasyBib help generate citations.

How do I cite an AI tool?

Follow your style's guidelines for software or digital tools.

Can I use footnotes instead of in-text citations?

Only if your style (like Chicago) requires or allows it.

Where can I find a full style guide?

Visit official style websites or your library’s citation guide.

Academic integrity means being honest, responsible, and ethical in all your academic work. It involves giving proper credit through citations, avoiding plagiarism, and completing assignments independently unless collaboration is allowed. Upholding academic integrity not only builds your credibility as a learner but also reflects your commitment to genuine learning and personal growth. Always strive to do your best work the right way.

Your future self will thank you! 🎓📚

Academic Integrity

How do I avoid plagiarism?

Always cite sources, paraphrase properly, and use plagiarism checkers.

What counts as self-plagiarism?

Reusing your own work without citation or permission.

Can I reuse content from previous assignments?

Only with permission or citation—check your institution's policy.

What’s considered cheating in writing?

Copying, outsourcing, or falsifying content is considered cheating.

Can I use AI writing tools ethically?

Yes, if disclosed and not used to misrepresent your authorship.

Do I need to cite lecture notes?

Yes, especially if directly quoting or referencing specific ideas.

Is collaboration allowed?

Only if explicitly permitted by your instructor or course policy.

What are the penalties for plagiarism?

They range from grade penalties to academic suspension.

Can paraphrasing still be plagiarism?

Yes, if it's too close to the original or not cited.

How do I paraphrase effectively?

Read, understand, and rewrite in your own words—then cite.

What is common knowledge?

Facts known by many and not needing citation—e.g., “The Earth revolves around the sun.”

Should I cite general ideas?

If the idea is specific to an author, yes. If it’s general or common, no.

Is using writing services allowed?

Only for editing or feedback—never for full authorship.

Can I be penalized for unintentional plagiarism?

Yes, intent doesn’t always matter—always double-check citations.

How can I check my work?

Use plagiarism checkers, citation tools, and peer feedback.

Writing Process & Tips

Where do I start with an essay?

Begin with understanding the prompt and outlining your points.

How do I write a strong thesis?

Make it specific, arguable, and reflective of your essay's focus.

What's a good essay structure?

Intro, thesis, body paragraphs with evidence, and a conclusion.

How long should a paragraph be?

Typically 4–6 sentences, focusing on one idea.

How do I transition between paragraphs?

Use linking phrases like “In addition,” or “However.”

What is passive vs active voice?

Active: "The student wrote the paper." Passive: "The paper was written by the student."

Should I use first-person voice?

Depends on your assignment; it's okay in reflections but not formal reports.

How do I proofread effectively?

Take breaks, read aloud, and use grammar tools.

What's the best way to conclude?

Summarize your argument and restate the thesis in a fresh way.

How can I make writing more concise?

Avoid redundancy and use strong verbs.

What tools can help me write?

Grammarly, Hemingway, Google Docs, and citation managers.

How do I write an introduction?

Start with a hook, provide background, and end with a thesis.

What is a topic sentence?

The main idea of a paragraph—usually the first sentence.

Can I use bullet points?

Only if appropriate—like in outlines or technical writing.

How do I develop ideas?

Ask questions, research, and explore multiple perspectives.
